# NMI AI Competition Rules

## Overview

The NMI AI Competition is a national AI competition consisting of two phases: a pre-competition phase and a main competition phase. Teams compete across multiple AI challenges for a total prize pool of 1,000,000 NOK.

## Eligibility

Participants must meet the eligibility requirements as defined by the competition organizers. All team members must be registered on the competition platform prior to the competition start.

## Team Structure

Teams may consist of one or more members. Each participant may only belong to one team. Team composition is locked once the main competition begins.

## Prize Pool

The total prize pool is **1,000,000 NOK** (one million Norwegian kroner), distributed across the competition tasks.

## Tasks

The competition includes multiple tasks spanning different AI domains. Each task has its own scoring criteria, submission format, and evaluation methodology. Tasks are released according to the competition schedule.

## Scoring

Each task is scored independently. Scores are **normalized to a 0-100 scale** for each task. A team's final score is the **average of their 3 normalized task scores**. This ensures balanced performance across all challenges is rewarded.

## Prize Eligibility

To be eligible for prizes, teams must:

1. **Vipps**: Have a valid Vipps account for prize distribution.
2. **Public Repository**: Make their competition code available in a public repository after the competition concludes.

Teams that do not meet both requirements will be disqualified from prize consideration.

## Fair Play

- Teams must not interfere with other teams' submissions or the competition infrastructure.
- Sharing solutions between teams during the competition is prohibited.
- All code submitted must be the team's own work or properly attributed open-source code.
- Automated attacks on the competition platform are strictly forbidden.

## Monitoring

The competition organizers reserve the right to monitor submissions, code, and platform activity to ensure compliance with competition rules. Suspicious activity may result in disqualification.

## Intellectual Property

All competition submissions are licensed under the **MIT License**. By participating, teams agree that their submitted code will be made available under this license.

## Jury

The competition jury has final authority on all matters related to scoring, rule interpretation, and disqualification. Jury decisions are final and not subject to appeal.

## Communication

Official communication channels will be used for announcements, rule clarifications, and support. Teams are responsible for monitoring these channels throughout the competition.

## Amendments

The organizers reserve the right to amend these rules at any time. Teams will be notified of any changes through official communication channels.

## Pre-Competition Rules

<div class="glass rounded-2xl text-card-foreground transition-all hover:shadow-lg">

<div class="p-6 space-y-6 pt-6">

<div class="section">

### 1. Overview

The pre-competition phase runs from February 20 to March 16, 2026 at 03:00 CET. During this period, registered teams can familiarize themselves with the platform and compete in the Grocery Bot Challenge. The winning team will be awarded 10,000 NOK and presented on stage at the NM i AI 2026 kickoff event in Oslo on March 19, 2026.

</div>

<div class="section">

### 2. The Grocery Bot Challenge

Teams connect an AI agent to a grocery store simulation via WebSocket. The agent controls a swarm of worker bots to pick and deliver grocery orders. Performance is measured by the total number of items successfully delivered.

The challenge has five difficulty levels:

- **Easy** — 12x10 grid, 1 bot, 300 rounds
- **Medium** — 16x12 grid, 3 bots, 300 rounds
- **Hard** — 22x14 grid, 5 bots, 300 rounds
- **Expert** — 28x18 grid, 10 bots, 300 rounds
- **Nightmare** — 30x18 grid, 20 bots, 3 drop-off zones, 500 rounds

Each game has a wall clock time limit of 2 minutes (5 minutes for Nightmare). Only the best score per map is retained on the leaderboard.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="glass rounded-2xl text-card-foreground transition-all hover:shadow-lg">

<div class="p-6 space-y-6 pt-6">

<div class="section">

### 3. Eligibility

Participation in the pre-competition is open to all registered teams. Teams may have between 1 and 5 members. Each person may only be a member of one team.

Participants must be at least 15 years of age at the time of registration. There are no identity verification requirements for the pre-competition phase.

</div>

<div class="section">

### 4. Rate Limits

Games are initiated through the Challenge page. The following rate limits apply per team:

- 60-second cooldown between games
- Maximum 40 games per hour
- Maximum 300 games per day

Game tokens are single-use. Each token can only be used to start one game session.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="glass rounded-2xl text-card-foreground transition-all hover:shadow-lg">

<div class="p-6 space-y-6 pt-6">

<div class="section">

### 5. Leaderboard & Scoring

The leaderboard updates in real-time as new scores are recorded. Only the highest score per difficulty level is retained for each team. The total score is the sum of best scores across all difficulty levels.

The final leaderboard snapshot at March 16, 2026 at 03:00 CET is the official result. No games completed after the deadline will be counted.

</div>

<div class="section">

### 6. Prize

The team at the top of the leaderboard when the pre-competition ends wins 10,000 NOK. The prize will be awarded on stage at the NM i AI 2026 kickoff event in Oslo on March 19, 2026.

In case of a tie, the team that achieved their score first wins. Prize money is split equally among team members who were registered on the team at the time the winning score was recorded.

Prize payment will be made by bank transfer after the kickoff event and prize handover. The organizers will contact the winning team's captain to arrange payment details. Winners are responsible for any applicable tax obligations arising from the prize under Norwegian law. The organizers do not withhold or cover taxes on behalf of recipients.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="glass rounded-2xl text-card-foreground transition-all hover:shadow-lg">

<div class="p-6 space-y-6 pt-6">

<div class="section">

### 7. Fair Play & Prohibited Conduct

The spirit of this competition is to build the most effective AI agent through clever algorithmic design, smarter bot coordination, and better decision-making. Optimizing your agent's strategy within the rules of the game is not only permitted — it is the point.

The following actions are strictly prohibited and may result in warnings, suspension, or permanent ban at the discretion of the organizers:

- Exploiting bugs or unintended vulnerabilities in the platform, game server, or evaluation system — as distinct from legitimate in-game strategy
- Attempting to circumvent rate limits or authentication controls
- Using multiple accounts or teams to gain an unfair advantage
- Automated abuse or actions that degrade platform performance for other participants
- Reverse-engineering the game server internals for purposes other than improving your agent's in-game performance
- Sharing account credentials with non-team members

If you are uncertain whether a technique is permitted, contact the organizers before using it. If in doubt, the jury's interpretation is final.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="glass rounded-2xl text-card-foreground transition-all hover:shadow-lg">

<div class="p-6 space-y-6 pt-6">

### 8. Platform Availability

The organizers will make all reasonable efforts to keep the platform available throughout the competition period. However, the organizers accept no liability for downtime, interruptions, latency, or technical failures caused by infrastructure, third-party services, or circumstances outside their control.

No extensions, score adjustments, or compensatory games will be granted due to platform unavailability, except at the sole discretion of the jury in cases of prolonged, verified, and widespread outages.

</div>

<div class="section">

### 9. Intellectual Property

Participants retain full ownership of all code, models, and solutions they develop during the competition. By participating, teams grant the organizers a non-exclusive, royalty-free right to reference their participation and results in public communications (e.g., announcing winners, leaderboard highlights). No source code will be published or shared without explicit consent from the team.

</div>

</div>

<div class="glass rounded-2xl text-card-foreground transition-all hover:shadow-lg">

<div class="p-6 space-y-6 pt-6">

<div class="section">

### 10. Enforcement & Bans

The organizers reserve the right to ban teams or individual members from the platform at any time for violations of these rules. Bans may be temporary or permanent. Banned teams will have their scores removed from the leaderboard. Enforcement decisions are made at the sole discretion of the jury.

</div>

<div class="section">

### 11. Appeals

Teams may appeal enforcement decisions, disqualifications, or scoring disputes by submitting a written appeal to <a href="mailto:erik@astarconsulting.no" class="underline">erik@astarconsulting.no</a> within 24 hours of being notified of the decision.

Appeals must include the team name, the decision being contested, and a clear explanation of the grounds for appeal. The jury will review all appeals and respond within a reasonable time. The jury's decision on appeal is final.

Appeals are available for enforcement and disqualification decisions only. Scoring disputes arising from technical failures (see Section 8) are not subject to appeal unless a systemic platform error is demonstrated.

</div>

<div class="section">

### 12. Jury

The jury consists of Erik and Mikael from Astar. The jury is responsible for interpreting these rules, resolving disputes, and making all final decisions regarding scores, disqualifications, and prize eligibility. All jury decisions are binding.

</div>

</div>

</div>

<div class="glass rounded-2xl text-card-foreground transition-all hover:shadow-lg">

### 13. Code of Conduct

Participants are expected to engage respectfully with other teams and organizers. Harassment, hate speech, or disruptive behavior in any platform channel — including the official Slack workspace — will not be tolerated and may result in immediate disqualification.

</div>

<div class="section">

### 14. Data & Privacy

Submissions, game replays, and associated metadata may be analyzed for quality assurance and anti-cheating purposes. Personal data is handled in accordance with Norwegian data protection regulations (GDPR). Participants under the age of 18 may be subject to additional data handling considerations under applicable law.

Game replays are stored and may be publicly viewable.

</div>

<div class="section">

### 15. Amendments

These rules may be updated at any time prior to the close of the pre-competition. Changes will be communicated through the official Slack workspace and platform announcements. Continued participation after a rule change constitutes acceptance of the updated rules.

</div>

</div>

</div>

This document covers the pre-competition phase only (February 20 – March 16, 2026). These rules do not govern the main competition beginning March 19, 2026, which will be subject to a separate ruleset.

### Fair Play & Prohibited Conduct

</div>

</div>

<div class="p-6 pt-0 space-y-5">

The purpose of this competition is to build effective AI solutions through genuine technical skill. The use of AI coding assistants (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, etc.), publicly available models, datasets, research papers, and open-source libraries is explicitly permitted and encouraged.

The following actions are strictly prohibited:

<div class="space-y-4">

<div class="space-y-2">

#### 10.1 Collusion & Solution Sharing

- <span class="text-muted-foreground mt-1 shrink-0">•</span>Sharing code, model weights, trained models, or task-specific solutions between teams — whether directly, through intermediaries, or through any public or private channel
- <span class="text-muted-foreground mt-1 shrink-0">•</span>Sharing competition-specific observations that provide a competitive advantage between teams
- <span class="text-muted-foreground mt-1 shrink-0">•</span>Coordinating submissions, strategies, or division of labor between teams

Discussing general AI/ML techniques, publicly available research, or topics not derived from participation in this competition is permitted. The line is drawn at sharing information that provides a competitive advantage specific to this competition's tasks.

</div>

<div class="space-y-2">

#### 10.2 Identity & Account Manipulation

- <span class="text-muted-foreground mt-1 shrink-0">•</span>Participating on more than one team, directly or through proxies
- <span class="text-muted-foreground mt-1 shrink-0">•</span>Creating additional accounts or teams to gain extra submissions, queries, or game attempts
- <span class="text-muted-foreground mt-1 shrink-0">•</span>Transferring, selling, or sharing team credentials or platform access
- <span class="text-muted-foreground mt-1 shrink-0">•</span>Using Vipps verification belonging to a person not genuinely participating on the team

</div>

<div class="space-y-2">

#### 10.3 Platform Abuse

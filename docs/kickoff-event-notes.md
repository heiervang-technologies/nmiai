## Keynote: Nicolai Tangen (CEO, Norges Bank Investment Management)

Tangen delivered the opening keynote. Key quotes and insights:

### On AI Adoption at the Oil Fund
- "We are all in. 100% are on Claude. 70% are on Claude Code. And we weren't even a technology company."
- Claims **20% productivity increase in one year** across the organization
- "If you have that for a little more than two years, you're up at 50%. If you're 50% more than your competitors, they can never catch you."

### On Technology Acceleration
- Drew analogy to Olympics: humans improve 1% per cycle, technology improves 1000x per cycle
- "From one Olympiad to the next: 1000x. Then 1000x again. That's a billion times complexity."
- "We have never seen a technology curve like we have now."

### On AI Necessity
- "Top leaders I talk to call this Red Alert."
- "The train is leaving. You can't stand there with your hat in your hand. That hand should be on your keyboard."
- "If you think you're done learning, you're not unlearned -- you're finished."

### On Organizational Transformation
- IT went from "the basement" to the strategic planning table
- Students go from onboarding to production in 35 minutes (vs. years before)
- "The boring things we used to do -- we don't do boring things anymore. Now we only do fun things."

### On Competition Prep
- Admitted using Claude to prepare for his NRK Debatten appearance later that evening
- "I got the ten questions they think I'll be asked. I'm betting I'll get seven of the ten."

## Tripletex Presentation - Jan Strandbakke

Jan Strandbakke leads 135 developers at Tripletex.

### Key Facts Shared
- **Tripletex is Norway's most-used accounting system** (referred to as "ledende okonomisystem")
- **160,000 business customers**, 400,000 monthly users
- **27 million invoices** handled
- **20 years** in the market

### AI at Tripletex Today
- **Support assistant handles 70%+ of all support** (email, phone, everything) with 96%+ accuracy
- **10+ AI agents in production** handling payroll insights, reporting, business tips
- **63% of all code produced in February 2026 was written by AI** (primarily Claude), across 135 developers
- Goal: **100% AI-generated code by end of 2026** ("more a mindset than a metric -- whether it's 80 or 100%")

### Quotes
- "AI is not optional. You have to go all in."
- "You're not a software developer anymore. You're a product developer."
- "If you don't catch up as a developer, you're outdated tomorrow."
- On the new Claude 4.5 and 4.6 models: "Even the passive, negative senior developer said 'holy shit, this nails the job I'm used to doing.'"

### Relevance to Task
The accounting agent task reflects real Tripletex challenges. Contestants build agents that perform actual operations in their system (employee management, invoicing, MVA, etc.), mirroring what Tripletex is doing internally with their 10+ production agents.

## NorgesGruppen Data Presentation - Tom Daniel Sivertsen

Tom Daniel Sivertsen leads the AI Lab at NorgesGruppen Data.

### Key Facts Shared
- **NorgesGruppen is Norway's largest grocery retailer**
- ~50,000 employees, 2,100 stores nationwide
- **8.3 million customers per week**
- **1 million order lines per day** leaving warehouses
- Sustainable production, electric trucks, sea drones, own energy production

### AI Focus Areas
- **Efficient value chains**: Right product, right place, right time, right quantity
- **Unmanned stores**: Keeping stores open in rural areas without full-time staff
- **Frictionless shopping experience**: Seamless in-store experience
- **Food waste reduction**: Already reduced 50%+ since 2015, targeting further reduction
- **Climate neutral operations by 2030**
- **Computer vision in self-checkout kiosks**
- **Classical ML for demand forecasting**

### Vision
- "AI is not just what happens on a computer screen -- it's also in the physical value chain"
- Emphasis on digitizing the physical world with cheap cameras and optimizing from there
- "Large companies that have been around a long time must adapt and be ahead of the game"

### Relevance to Task
The object detection task directly maps to NorgesGruppen's planogram compliance use case: detecting products on shelves, identifying misplaced items, spotting empty shelves. This is active production work at NorgesGruppen, not an academic exercise.

## Astar Island Context

Presented by Erik Nymo Bohne with a cinematic trailer video.

### Design Philosophy
- Originated from combining A-Star (pathfinding algorithm, the company's namesake) with the grocery store concept
- Initially intended as the main competition task, repurposed as pre-game (Grocery Bot) and then reimagined as a Norse civilization simulator
- "We can't just have industry problems. We need to have fun too."

### Simulation Parallels
- Norse civilization development as a statistical prediction problem
- Erik drew explicit parallel to epidemiology: "Like during corona, when you needed to figure out how the virus spread" -- same kind of probabilistic modeling
- Stochastic simulation with exploration-exploitation tradeoff under budget constraints
